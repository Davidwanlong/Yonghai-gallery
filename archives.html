In September 2001, at the invitation of Liu Gang, the general manager of "Shanghai Painters' Village", the Hebei Oil Painters' Gallery moved to Shanghai and settled in Room 2003 of the Painters' Village. The works of the Hebei Oil Painters' Gallery were displayed in the exhibition room at the entrance of the Painters' Village exhibition hall, which was particularly eye-catching. The works of Ming Jing were specifically requested by Liu Gang and were soon purchased and collected by friends from Singapore. In November of the same year, it participated in the "Shanghai Art Fair" and the "Gallery Invitation Exhibition of Shanghai Art Fair", and has participated for three consecutive years. Many works have been collected at home and abroad.

This is the promotional page of the Hebei Oil Painters' Gallery during its relocation to Shanghai in 2000. It includes Wu Guanzhong's "Dawn of Weishan Lake" created in 1962, which was soon purchased and collected by Taiwan; Fei Zheng's "Round Lantern Night", which was also later collected by Taiwan; Sun Gang's representative work "Dam. Scenery 2"; Xu Fuhou's representative work "Man and Fabric"; Ming Jing's work "The Man Playing the Guitar", which was later purchased and collected by a local in Shanghai; Huo Genzhong's "Corn Field"; Jia Xiangguo's "Loess Space"; Lu Shukai's "Dusk";

Niu Yungong's masterpiece "Grand Flourishing Age", which has been privately purchased and collected by a person in Shijiazhuang; Gao Yingjin's "Black Bottle"; Wang Wensheng's "Hengshanling"; Nan Fang's "Portrait"; Zhao Wenhua's "Scenery"; Wang Tao's "General Chair"; Huang Junhui's "Fish" and Wang Yonghai's "Crowd on the Beach".
